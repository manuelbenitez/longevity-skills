# Turmeric

A golden-yellow rhizome used for thousands of years in Indian and Southeast Asian cooking. Turmeric's active compound, curcumin, is one of the most studied natural anti-inflammatory agents in nutrition science.

## Why It Matters for Longevity

Chronic low-grade inflammation is a driver of nearly every age-related disease: heart disease, cancer, Alzheimer's, diabetes. Curcumin directly targets this process.

The mechanism is specific: curcumin blocks IkB kinase, which prevents the NF-kB protein from entering the cell nucleus and switching on genes that produce inflammatory cytokines like TNF-alpha and IL-6. A meta-analysis of 8 randomized controlled trials found that curcumin supplementation significantly reduced C-reactive protein (CRP), a key inflammation marker, by an average of 2.2 mg/L.

There is also promising (though less conclusive) evidence for neuroprotection. Populations with high dietary turmeric intake show lower rates of Alzheimer's disease. Curcumin crosses the blood-brain barrier and appears to reduce amyloid-beta plaque accumulation, though large-scale human trials are still needed.

## How to Use It

**Daily amount:** 1-2 teaspoons ground turmeric, or a thumb-sized piece of fresh root.

**Preparation matters.** Curcumin is fat-soluble and poorly absorbed on its own (less than 1% bioavailability). Two things fix this:

1. **Cook with fat.** Olive oil, coconut oil, or ghee. The curcuminoids dissolve into the fat and get absorbed through the intestinal wall.
2. **Add black pepper.** Just a pinch. Piperine in black pepper inhibits the liver enzyme that breaks down curcumin, boosting absorption by up to 2000%.

Heat also activates certain curcuminoids, so cooking is better than raw consumption for this particular ingredient.

## What to Pair It With

| Ingredient | Why | Tradition |
|-----------|-----|-----------|
| Black pepper | 2000% bioavailability boost via piperine | Indian (Ayurvedic trikatu) |
| Olive oil | Fat-soluble absorption + its own anti-inflammatory oleocanthal | Mediterranean |
| Ginger | Complementary anti-inflammatory pathways (COX-2 + NF-kB) | Indian, Southeast Asian |
| Chickpeas | Protein + fiber vehicle, classic pairing | Indian |
| Coconut milk | Fat for absorption + creamy base for curries | Thai, Indonesian |
